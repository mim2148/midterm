What is body language?

It's a language in which physical behavior, as opposed to words, is used to express feelings. Per Professor Mehrabian communication is only 7% verbal and 93% non-verbal ( 55% is body language and 38% istone of voice ). Such behavior includes:

  • facial expressions
  • body posture
  • gestures
  • eye movement
  • touch
  • the use of space
    •

Body language complements verbal communication and also adds information about the person displaying it. Therefore in order to collect more information about the presidential candidates it is convenient to recognize and interpret the body language displayed by both, especifically the body posture and the use of space.
Body movements can distract from the message. Just as natural body movements can strengthen the message, unnecessary movements can distract from delivery. Here are some examples of movements which distract:

  • Swaying back and forth:If you sway back and forth at the stage in a pattern without purpose, the audience may follow the movement rather than the message.

  • Pacing from one side to other:If you pace from one side of the room to the other meaninglessly, the audience will follow the movement.

  • Onstage Lurking: a classic way to convey intimidation,distress. watch
